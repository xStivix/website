AI WORKFLOW

Generative AI

When working with AI in video production, generative AI is the most transformative innovation, fundamentally changing how content is created. I prefer an image-to-video approach since it offers the most creative control. With today’s tools, and enough refernece images of objects and characters it is already possible to generate nearly photorealistic images and even full video sequences that, with the right workflow, editing and time, can look almost indistinguishable from real productions. The process still follows classic filmmaking principles: each shot is planned in terms of subject, lighting, camera, lens, setting, and action. I start with a base image, refine it with sketches, 3D models, or references, and then polish it through editing and color grading. Every single shot is created individually, which takes time but ensures maximum control and consistency. This image then becomes the foundation for the AI video model, and then the final AI video output is completed with manual editing and finishing touches one last time.

October 2025

Sora2 Showcase

This video was created with Sora 2, which as of October 1st, 2025 is still in an early stage and only really supports text-to-video at 480p with a visible watermark. Compared to other video models it has major limitations in control, consistency, and overall usability, making it impractical for real-world projects for now. It only allows one reference image, so either you go for a consistent scenery or a consistent character, both is tricky but solvable through prompting. Still, its progress is impressive: it shows a stronger grasp of physics, can generate multiple consistent camera angles in one scene, and even includes built-in audio. The whole process took me just about five hours, and aside from my small concept, manual editing and music mixing, the visuals and most of the engine sounds came directly from Sora 2. This is not a polished edit, simply a demo showcase. Despite its flaws, Sora already is a new state-of-the-art benchmark in physics, audio and scene consistency within its 10-second generation window. This video got over 1 million views in just one day across all platforms.

December 2024

Ultimate Drive

This is my first ever proof of concept based on AI-generated footage using an early image-to-video workflow, similar to today’s best practices but with much less control. This clip focuses more on the results of traditional editing craftsmanship than on raw AI output alone. This is the result of over 80 hours of hands-on work: generating images and videos, editing, sound design, VFX and color grading. Images were created with MidJourney 6.1, refined in Photoshop and Magnific AI, animated with Kling 1.6 AI and Veo2, and finalized in After Effects with flares, flames, lighting effects, camera shake, and a 3D Porsche logo. Final polish and upscaling were done with Topaz AI. Below is a before-and-after comparison aswell as the final clip which generated roughly 1 million views across all platforms.

TEch insights

Video Editing

My workflow is built around mainly industry-standard Adobe Programs.
For AI-assisted tasks, I use a wide spread collection of tools for image & video generation as well as editing. My main OS is Windows, but I’m also comfortable on macOS.

Color Management

I can work with all major camera systems: ARRI Alexa (Log C), RED Komodo & DSMC2 (REDCODE RAW / Log3G10), Blackmagic RAW & Sony S-Log. My pipeline is designed around Rec. 709 (Log to Rec 709 conversion).

Programs & Tools

My main program is Adobe After Effects but I also work with Premiere, Photoshop, and Davinci. In addition, I use a wide range of AI tools, including Midjourney, Runway, Topaz, Kling, ChatGPT, Gemini, Veo and others.

Delivery Formats

I deliver in all common industry formats, including ProRes 422 HQ, ProRes 4444, and H.264/HEVC (up to 4K resolution). Depending on the project's needs, I also provide custom outputs for social media platforms and web use.

Miscellaneous
